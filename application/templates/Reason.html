Reasons behind playing video games vary greatly from person-to-person. For the purpose of this project, the reasons were generalized and divided into six sub-groups: relaxing, winning, improving, having fun, all of the above and others. While the difference in gaming anxiety among the different afforementioned playstyles is very miniscule, the data shows that those who play to have fun are less affected by gamer anxiety compared to those who play to win. The overly competitive nature and expectation of winning can be the cause of higher anxiety levels among gamers.
